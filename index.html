openBarter

openBarter is the model of a barter market of fungible values. It is organized as a central limit order book (CLOB), accepting orders and producing movements.

Gold is fungible. Silver is fungible; that is, these metals are both so homogeneous that,if I get a pound of pure gold, for example, it is indifferent to me whether it be this pound or that pound,one is as good as another.

openBarter allows cyclic exchange between up to 64 partners in a single transaction. The combinatorics is so rich that for a reasonable diversity of qualities, openBarter is nearly as liquid as a regular CLOB.

Each value has a pecular quality and quantity. The value is only measured by the quantity without any reference to a price or reference to a monetary standard. This measurement is used to perform competition between orders.

openBarter uses postgreSQL, the linux of databases. A special extension of this database allows fast execution of the primitives of the model.

Principles

The barter market accepts exchange orders of the form:

I propose a value in exchange of a value of an other quality for a minimum quantity

submitted by the owner of the value proposed.

The market finds potential exchange cycles with two partners or more. Agreements can be formed from them, defined by a set of movements where each partner provides a quantity to an other and receives at the same time a value of the quality required by it's order. By allowing more than two partners in a cycle the liquidity of the market is not limited by the double coincidence of wants problem.

For an order, we define ω as a ratio between provided quantity and minimum required quantity. For a cycle formed by several orders where quality offered and required match, we compute an Ω as the product of ω of orders of the cycle, and see that it is a dimensionless quantity. When Ω is lower than 1, the common will to exchange is not sufficient to find an agreement between partners due to minimum quantities required. When Ω is 1 it is easy to find an agreement than observe the maximum ratio ω defined by the minimum quantity required. When Ω is greater than 1 the excess Ω-1 can be shared fairly for the benefit of partners to form the exchange. When an owner submits an order, that's because he considers that the value expected is more useful that the one he owns, and ω measures how much this exchange would be useful for him. For a given cycle, Ω is proportional to any ω of it's orders since Ω is their product. In other words Ω is an aggregate measuring how much the exchange is globally useful for the partners of the cycle. Ω is dimensionless and equal or greater than 1 when an exchange is possible; it can be used to compare the usefulness of cycles. This comparison defines a maximum representing the most useful exchange cycle. It can showed that the choice of the cycle having Ω maximum is the same as what would be obtained with the best price rule when applied to bilateral exchange using prices. By maximizing Ω the market meets the goal of utilitarians by maximizing utility but with a definition of utility that is independent of any currency.

This market proceeds as a regular CLOB - by processing orders one after the other. We describe here what is common between these markets. The input of the market is a flow of orders and it's output is a flow of movements. It records unmatched orders in an order book. When a new order is submitted, a competition is performed between potential cycles created by the new order and pending orders in the book to choose the cycle that will form the exchange. If no matching is found, the new order is added to the book. Otherwise, movements forming the exchange are produced from the selected cycle and the value offered by matched orders are decreased of the value exchanged. If some cycles remain the competition is repeated as long as the new order is not exhausted.

The difference between this barter market and a regular CLOB is only that 1) exchange cycles can be non-bilateral 2) competition is performed with Ω instead of price.

Origins and goals

The difficulty of driving a system growns as it's complexity, due to the diversity of command signals that need to be carefully adjusted. Mankind is in charge of restoring the ecologic stability of the planet, but ecological economy is driven by a mono-dimensional signal that is money. openBarter provides a mean to control each dimension of the control signal independently, and to build market instruments independantly of currencies.
