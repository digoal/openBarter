openBarter

openBarter is the model of a barter market of fungible values. It is organized as a central limit order book.

Gold is fungible. Silver is fungible; that is, these metals are both so homogeneous that,if I get a pound of pure gold, for example, it is indifferent to me whether it be this pound or that pound,one is as good as another.

Each fungible value has a pecular quality and quantity. An order is an unilateral commitment to provide a fungible in exchange of an other.

The ratio between quantity offered and quantity required is used instead of a price to perform competition between orders.

openBarter allows cyclic exchange between up to 8 partners in a single transaction.

The combinatorics is so rich that for a reasonable diversity of qualities, openBarter is nearly as liquid as a regular market.

The market does not require a central value standard to express prices.

Mathematic principles of openbarter are detailed on this paper. openBarter uses postgreSQL, the linux of databases. A special extension of this database allows fast execution of the primitives of the model.

Origins

The difficulty of driving a system growns as it's complexity, due to the diversity of command signals that need to be carefully adjusted. Mankind is in charge of restoring the ecologic stability of the planet, but ecological economics is driven by a mono-dimensional signal that is money. openBarter provides a mean to control each dimension of the control signal independently.
